me(l) in a nutshell

My full name is Melanie Joy Tabroff (I usually go by Mel), and I love climbing trees!

…actually, what makes me most happy is just being outside whenever I have the chance. When I’m not working on design projects, you can find me running along Hudson River Park, citi-biking, or power walking through my favorite neighborhoods, like a true New-Yorka!

My Journey— why UX?

About a year ago I made the decision to enroll in an online intensive program for UX Design and Front-end development, and since then I’ve been doing freelance UX research and marketing for an ed tech startup.

What led me to make the decision to pursue UX was simple: I spent a good amount of time working alongside designers and developers at my previous jobs (at both a real estate startup and creative agency), and I always felt drawn to their work. The more I researched, the more excited I became about UX.

What solidified the decision for me, though, was realizing that there were aspects of UX I was already doing in my previous roles (problem-solving wasn’t anything new to me. Adobe Suite, on the otherhand, was…).

I’m also naturally curious about people and how they think, so the ability to design products and experiences for people really gets me pumped!

My philosophy— Hygge

When I traveled to Copenhagen I discovered Hygge [‘HOO-gah”], which loosely translates to “coziness.” From what I understand, Hygge is all about designing your life to support happiness, good health, and wellbeing. I’m a big Hygge enthusiast and try to make a daily effort to do the things that inspire me and make me happy. This means drinking a good cup of coffee and working on projects that I believe in.
